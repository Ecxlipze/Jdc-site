The pursuit of knowledge should not be paused by hardship.

When classrooms close or become out of reach, learning should not disappear. For many children, distance, illness, poverty, or damaged infrastructure can interrupt education for months or even years. The Madad Distant Learning Program exists to keep learning alive during these breaks.

This program does not replace formal education systems. Instead, it helps children and learners stay connected to learning until classrooms become accessible again.

What This Program Supports

The program provides carefully planned learning support that helps learners continue at their own pace, including:

  • Access to selected learning content that supports basic educational needs
  • Offline learning resources for areas with limited or unreliable internet access
  • Guided learning support through trusted schools and community institutions
  • Simple learning schedules that respect local realities and family responsibilities

All learning support is designed to be safe, neutral, age-appropriate, and free from exploitation.

How Support Is Delivered

Support under this program is delivered through:

  • Schools
  • Community learning centers
  • Clinics that support education services
  • Verified partner institutions

Madad Farma does not deliver unsupervised learning directly to individuals. Learning access is always facilitated through institutions to ensure safety, accountability, and long-term continuity.
